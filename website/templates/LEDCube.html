 LED Cube

This may be my most treasured project that I have completed as it was my first-ever project related to my field. While it is simple by design, I was extremely intrigued by the concept of creating something from scratch at the time. When I completed the project, I was thoroughly mind blown by the idea that I was the creator of the flashing cube before me. The LED Cube opened a whole new world of electronics to me and really inspired me to continue to work with microcontrollers.


 Technical Details

  • Ran the program from an Arduino Uno. Other hardware consisted of: 4x4x4 (64) blue leds, and 4 resistors for every level of of the cube, where every level was grounded. The bottom 16 pins of the led structure were connected to 16 different gpio pins from the Uno.

  • Since I knew nothing about programming at the time, I tweaked some online source code which provided a series of lighting effects.

  • A ton of delicate soldering was required for this project. This was a challenge for me as I was just a beginner. I was also using lead-free solder at the time which subsequently made the process a lot harder.
