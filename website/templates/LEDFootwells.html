 LED Car Footwells

This was a short and simple project that once again involves LEDs. I enjoy working with LEDs as they are easy to include in new projects while allowing me to use microcontrollers and sharpen my soldering skills. And of course, they are fun to look at! For this project, the LEDs are located in the footwells of the driver, passenger and rear seats. First, I integrated them into the car system to be synchronous with the music played, thus requiring that I create a circuit. I later preferred that the LEDs stayed on constantly so I implemented a switch which can dial back to a constant stream of light whenever I wanted.


 Technical Details

  • Some of the hardware consisted of an Arduino Nano (to easily integrate the sound sensor into the system), a resistor, a capacitor, four 2-wire LED strips cut to proper length, and some wire.

  • The RC combination essentially created a lowpass filter, removing some of the higher-frequency noise the sensor picked up from the nearby speaker. The input signal from the sensor was then fed to an input pin on the Nano which provided a cleaner output for the LEDs.

  • The power supply to the circuit came directly from the car battery. I therefore, decided to go with 12v LEDs. Doing so meant I couldn't power the LEDs directly with the controller. Instead, I used an output pin on the controller to activate an NPN transistor that received the car battery's 12 volts into it's collector.
