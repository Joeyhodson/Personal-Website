 Electric Skateboard

Probably my most dangerous project to date, in which I modded my penny board into a remote-controlled, electric skateboard. This project required intuition and intensive mechanical work to fit every component under the board. Inspired by the many electric skateboards I saw on campus at UCF, I decided to embark on this project for fun, and I was definitely impressed by the speed of the board even though it was more of a novelty than something practical.


 Technical Details

  • My first goal was to find a motor that was able to propel me at a good working speed. Instead of going the brushless route, I went with a cheaper single-motor setup in which had a lot of torque. I decided with some 24VDC motor that I had found on eBay.

  • With an 11V LiPo battery I had laying around, I decided to purchase an identical battery and wire the two in series creating a strong enough voltage to get the motor moving at almost full throttle. A few pluses with LiPos is that first, they are rechargeable, and second, they are very lightweight.

  • Having to dremel into the board in a few places and mount a metal gear to one of the wheels, I attached a belt to the gear and strung it to the motor allowing a very tight connection between the two. This allowed the wheel to spin whenever the motor was activated.

  • To activate the motor remotely, I purchased a general, wireless DC relay that included a remote controller. After some tinkering I was able to properly wire everything to the circuit's terminals and activate the motor remotely.
