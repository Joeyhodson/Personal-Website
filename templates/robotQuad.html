 Robotic Quadruped

The robotic quadruped, a robotic spider with four legs. I always wanted to create some sort of robot and I had been playing around with servo motors at the time, so when I saw a similar quadruped online, I instantly knew I wanted to make one. I found an online tutorial that brought inspiration, however, I decided to tackle this project with little guidance to gain better experience. Going about this blindly, I decided to make the robot autonomous and unidirectional for simplicity. I still have a passion for robotics and may consider developing a second spider using a romote controller and stronger servos. My greatest struggle with this project was limiting stress on the servos and trying to decrease the robot's total body weight.


 Technical Details

  • I used an Arduino Nano as the controller since they are lighter than Unos. The controller was powered by a 9v lithium battery.
  • The robot had four 3-part moving limbs and therefore used 12 SG90 servo motors. I used 12 digital gpio pins and had a common power supply plane and ground plane that fed to each motor. I dealt with a lot of jitter in the motors so I included a few capacitors between the power/ground planes in which helped quite a bit.
  • The limbs were made from paint sticks in which I sanded and spray-painted black (I was on a budget). I cut 4 sticks each into 2 separate pieces, 4 femurs and 4 coxas.
  • To limit stress on the servos, I switched from 4 alkaline batteries to 4 lithium batteries, weighing roughly 60% of what the original AA batteries did. The AA batteries were wired in two sets of series connections, the two sets were then wired in parallel, outputting 3V to the servos.
  • The program essentially had a series of for loops within a while loop, each for loop being a servo motor's rotation. Having some new found knowledge in programming, I might implement this a little differently today.
